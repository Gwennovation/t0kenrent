# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Regular people who want to rent or list items peer-to-peer. They care about the rental — the camera, the vacation home, the tool — not the blockchain infrastructure enabling it. They open the app to transact, not to learn about BSV. Context: browsing listings on their phone or laptop, comparing a handful of options, deciding whether to commit a deposit. They expect the process to feel as familiar as any marketplace but faster and cheaper because there are no middlemen.

## Product Purpose

T0kenRent is a trustless P2P rental marketplace. Renters and listers transact directly; a 2-of-2 multisig escrow holds deposits until both parties sign off. The BSV blockchain records everything, but users never have to think about that. Success looks like: a renter finds an item, pays a small unlock fee to see details, deposits into escrow, completes the rental, and gets their deposit back — without a platform taking a cut or deciding disputes unilaterally.

## Brand Personality

Bold, technical, frontier. The product is genuinely novel infrastructure; the UI should reflect that quiet confidence — not through visual decoration but through precision, density, and the feeling that every interaction was considered. Linear and Vercel are the reference point: dark, opinionated, credible. The blockchain is a backend detail. The frontier is the experience.

## Anti-references

- Generic neon-on-black crypto: purple/teal gradients, laser grids, token hype, glow-on-everything. This is not DeFi.
- Airbnb clone: photo-heavy hero cards, warm pastels, "find your next adventure" copy. Too soft, loses the technical edge.

## Design Principles

1. **Blockchain as infrastructure, not identity.** BSV powers this; it does not define it. Users transact, not speculate. Keep wallet addresses, tx hashes, and chain status secondary unless the user needs them.
2. **Confident restraint over decoration.** Bold means decisive, not loud. Every element earns its place. No glow effects, no gradient text, no cards for the sake of cards.
3. **Trust through legibility.** Escrow state, deposit amounts, and rental status must be immediately readable. Ambiguity in financial UI erodes trust.
4. **Frontier through precision.** The product feels advanced because the details are right — spacing rhythm, type contrast, interaction timing — not because of visual effects.
5. **Dark as environment, not identity.** Dark mode is the default because this is a focused operational tool used across contexts. It is not a crypto signal.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Solid color contrast, full keyboard navigation, semantic HTML, screen reader support. Reduced motion preference should be respected for all transitions and animations.
